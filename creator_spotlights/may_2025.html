May 2025 Creator Spotlight - Ghourical

This month, our Artist of the Month is Ghourical!

Watch the YouTube short here.


Here's our interview with Ghourical:

- Can you introduce yourself and share a bit about your background?

Hello! My name is Elyssa, known as Ghourical on the internet. I am currently 18 years old attending university for Mechanical Engineering. I enjoy 3D modelling, 3D printing and bringing miscellaneous concepts to life using the power of technology.

- What inspired you to become an artist?

I was inspired to become an artist when looking at early 2000s video games, such as Super Mario Galaxy and Super Mario 64. I loved the trophies and objects in the game, and often wished to possess them in real life. It was also the appeal of certain visuals and aesthetics created by games, especially the presence of Nintendo during the prime of Frutiger Aero. I started to 3D model and create 3D art based off of these video games and my favorite aesthetic Frutiger Aero!

- How did you first discover Frutiger Aero as an aesthetic, and what drew you to it?

I first discovered Frutiger Aero as an aesthetic simply by living in it. I was around six years old during the prime of Frutiger Aero in the early 2010s, and had access to the internet as a child. The goldfish Softsoap containers, the clunky Windows 7, the Wii, the vivid imagery of the sky, the random generated backdrops of Windows 7 screensavers, Samsung Advertisements from Asia (I loved watching those!). When I rediscovered Frutiger Aero, it IMMEDIATELY resonated with me once I saw a few pictures, and unlocked a feeling of nostalgia for me. A feeling of nostalgia I had been looking for a long time.

- How would you describe your art style?

I describe my art style as calm and serene, typically made to make your mind reference moments of vivid Furtiger Aero nostalgia. My work is meant to make your mind turn blank, or really reflect on past experiences during your lifetime.

- Can you walk us through your creative process from idea to finished piece?

Most of my art pieces are created through Blender, a free multiplatform app for 3D modelling, rendering, simulation, etc. I usually create something original, or recreate an aesthetic, scene or object from a video game or piece of media from the 2000s. If I want to just create a visual, I go through a process of adding materials and rendering through layers in the software, or I prepare the 3D models for 3D printing in a slicer software.

- What tools or mediums do you prefer to use, and why?

I prefer to use Blender or a computer software to make my artwork, I’ve never been good at drawing and I like to bring my creations to life by 3D printing them!

- How do you overcome creative blocks?

I overcome creative blocks by just sitting at my computer and opening up the software to model. I look at Pinterest, asset libraries, or even in the Frutiger Aero Discord to check out some other inspirations in my work.

- What inspires your work? Are there specific themes, emotions, or stories you aim to convey?

What inspired my work is evoking thoughts/feelings out of another human. I aim to convey the peace and clarity that Frutiger Aero delivers when we are in need of finding calmness. When I create Frutiger Aero art, I try to convey key aspects of a few aesthetics from Frutiger Aero: glacier, water, and futuristic.

- Are there any artists, aesthetics, or movements that have influenced your style?

Staysie Atoms, Hung-Tung Lu, and the presence of technology and media in the early 2000s has influenced my style tremendously. Especially the UI layout of Nintendo gaming systems in the early 2000s, such as the Wii, DS & 3DS.

- What has been the most challenging piece you’ve created, and why?

The most challenging piece I’ve created were miniature trophies from the Special Cup of Mario Kart Wii. It was my first time getting the chance to operate my resin printer, so I had a lot of mechanical issues.

- Can you share a piece you’re especially proud of and why it’s significant to you?

A piece I'm proud of and why it's significant to me is the Frutiger Aero Dessert Plate. It has most of the components or notable visuals from frutiger aero all in one place, which is one of the aspects I strived for it to have.

- What do you hope people take away from your art?

I hope people come to an understanding of my appreciation for Frutiger Aero art, and how my art can make others feel.

- Do you have any upcoming projects or goals you’re excited about?

Not as of recently, as I am in college for Mechanical Engineering. As of right now, it’s taking up most of my time!

- Where can people find more of your work or support you?

I do have a Tik Tok i'm not really active on. My user is 3dghour

- If you could collaborate with any artist, living or dead, who would it be and why?

I would LOVEEE to collaborate with Hung Tung-Lu. He is one of the few artists that get the least recognition for contributing to Frutiger Aero art when it comes to China. We saw the most emergence of Frutiger Aero from places such as Taiwan, and Japan, but not nearly the same amount of the aesthetic coming from many Chinese advertisements or social media back then, so Hung Tung-Lu was a great inspiration for me when creating 3-D Frutiger Aero art.

- What’s a fun fact about you that most people wouldn’t know?

A fun fact about me that most people wouldn’t know is that I am obsessed with humanoid robotics.

- Do you have a favorite Frutiger Aero memory or moment?

My favorite Frutiger Aero moment/memory is from a Youtube video by a channel named ArpWire TV. They uploaded a Zen/Vaporwave Frutiger Aero visual/playlist that is to absolutely adore. My favorite part is the opening, where a man is sitting down in a chair that subsides into an ocean view as he continues to read.

- If you could describe your artistic journey in three words, what would they be?

Fearless, serene, unreal.

- What advice would you give to aspiring artists who are inspired by Frutiger Aero?

Be creative within your own means, not anyone else's. I get teased all the time by peers for my commitment to this art style.

- Is there anything else you’d like to share with the Frutiger Aero community?

Flourish and be great :)
