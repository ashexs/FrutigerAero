June 2026 Creator Spotlight - Bahpu

This month, our Artist of the Month is Bahpu!

Watch the previous YouTube short here. June is coming soon!


Here's our interview with Bahpu:

- Can you introduce yourself and share a bit about your background?

Hey, I’m Bahpu, I’m a 23 year old software engineer. For a bit of background I usually love doing creative stuff, I’ve messed around with loads of things like video editing, graphic design, web development, music production & more. I really wanna turn my hobbies into sources of income, that's one of my main plans. I will obtain financial freedom as soon as possible. Aside from that, in my spare time I love spending time with friends, playing video games, working out and I also follow boxing closely.

- What inspired you to become an artist?

I can’t really pinpoint a moment, but I’d say YouTube helped me discover my creative side a lot more. Being an optimistic, curious kid I loved recording and editing random videos with the hopes to become famous lmao, it ended up leading me down a lot of rabbit holes when it came to developing skills because I was forced to learn these things properly. Since then it’s snowballed.

- How did you first discover Frutiger Aero as an aesthetic, and what drew you to it?

My closest friend introduced the aesthetic to me (shoutout to u sami ilysm <3), I always loved how old operating systems like Windows Vista, 7, iOS 4 etc looked and missed everything about them, I wasn’t aware there was an aesthetic around it until she told me about it.

- How would you describe your art style?

For my Frutiger Aero websites/apps, I’d describe my artstyle as being a crossover between those old 2010s UIs and modern day UIs. My goal has always been to make apps that have the smoothness, clarity, good practices of today’s designs while trying to maintain that FA glossy look from the past. As much as I love how those old designs looked, many of them are outdated from a heuristic/usability side of things, I always try my best to find a way to actually make these designs totally viable to use today.

- Can you walk us through your creative process from idea to finished piece?

Most of the time I have a cool idea in my head, and just try my best to get it out in front of me, most of my best ideas come from before sleeping or in the shower lmao. Once I’m actually there creating the front-end of a website for example, I’ll loosely make a load of solid colour template boxes, slowly detail them, put placeholder content and focus on the layout/overall look, then start filling it in with the things I need and actually get to designing. From there it just snowballs really, I work really iteratively, constantly adding and changing things as I go along and it always ends up coming out well. Then I like doing one last check across the page/subpages to ensure it's consistent, brush up on usability and overall just polish it. For aurora/metro backgrounds that I’ve made I take heavy inspiration from Windows Vista backgrounds and just do whatever I think is cool, it's easy to get demotivated but most the cool stuff I make happens by accident, i’ll try something wack or use a different blending option and make something cool by chance! Just keep trying.

- What tools or mediums do you prefer to use, and why?

For video/graphic content I use Adobe (ew) products like Premiere Pro & Photoshop, for web development I tend to use React & Node.js, for music I use FL Studio. There’s not too much practical reason other than they’re all very powerful and I’ve used and mastered them for years, I don’t really have any reason to swap other than the coding frameworks depending on the task at hand. I think it’d be a hindrance to learn new software when these all allow me to get that creative stuff on my mind onto paper.

- How do you overcome creative blocks?

Creative blocks are a tricky one, it depends on what type of creative block it is. If you’re finding you’re literally burnt out, feeling like shit, then you gotta just take a step back and give yourself a break. There’s way too much pressure on “you need to keep going” in society but there’s certain times that just does NOT work at all, that kind of phrase doesn’t account for mental health issues or anything of the sort, so yeah definitely take a step back and focus on different things for a bit. Other than that, if I’m feeling motivated enough I’ll work on different parts of the project, take a small break to have a shower or something, or work on something entirely different and attack it the next day. Having a fresh mind with it helps so so much, even if you just take a 10 minute break from what you’re doing to go out and walk.

- What inspires your work? Are there specific themes, emotions, or stories you aim to convey?

Honestly not really, for FA work I just like looking at old pieces of software, art as well as new pieces being created by artists. The rest I sort of just come up with in my head! FA is super creative and speculative so it’s just whatever hits the spot for you really.

- Are there any artists, aesthetics, or movements that have influenced your style?

In terms of Frutiger Aero, not really. Just the aesthetic itself, its subaesthetics like Frutiger Aurora and all the software back then like Windows Vista/7.

- What role does the Frutiger Aero aesthetic play in your art?

Just how everything is so detailed, glossy, glassy, vibrant. I love how it looks, especially those old school icons they’re featured in pretty much everything I do around the aesthetic.

- What has been the most challenging piece you’ve created, and why?

Definitely the store website I’m currently working on, due to it being that the primary purpose is to provide a good shopping experience it requires a LOT of attention to readability, heuristics & overall usability. It’s definitely been a lot of fun though and I think it’s going very well.

- Can you share a piece you’re especially proud of and why it’s significant to you?

I’d say same as above but the Frutiger Aero Hub I recently released has been so cool, I’ve loved all the feedback and user suggestions, it’s always such a great feeling seeing people really enjoy what you’ve made after putting in so much work for so long. I hope to expand on it regularly and make it a huge library for resources, information and cool things related to all Frutiger subaesthetics. Also, just a cool checkpoint for people to visit from time to time.

- How has being part of the Frutiger Aero community influenced your artistic journey?

Massively! I don’t know the last time I made a website that wasn’t influenced by the community/aesthetic. I love seeing new things people post everyday and especially old things that they dig up because it provides me with loads of new ideas and inspiration.

- What do you hope people take away from your art?

I just hope they find it cool, inspiring, comforting and enjoyable. I also hope they appreciate my efforts to make Frutiger Aero layouts that also have a lot of modern design practices in place. It'll be cool to see people make some stuff themselves because they were inspired by what I did.

- Do you have any upcoming projects or goals you’re excited about?

Definitely, lots of things, but primarily my brand “Promised Future”, I hope to release it by the end of the year, I already have a lot of cool products planned including Y2K/Aero streetwear, reed diffusors with “aero” scents, body oils and more! Really looking forward to that, it's the reason I’m making the shop I mentioned above. I also really wanna continue uploading to youtube once i get my drive for it back.

- Where can people find more of your work or support you?

Frutiger Aero Hub, u/Bahpu_ (My reddit page), @Bahpu on discord and my youtube channels (AeroSource, Bahpu)

- If you could collaborate with any artist, living or dead, who would it be and why?

Not an artist, but I’d love to collaborate with the old FaZe members (like Teeqo, Apex, Rain). That's always been a dream of mine, and also a pipe dream would be to do something with John Cena. There’s a lot of cool ass people on this planet and I’d like to meet as many of them as possible and give them their flowers while they’re still here, so many of them have been instrumental to my goals and why I’m still going.

- What’s a fun fact about you that most people wouldn’t know?

Can’t really think of a super fun one on the top of my head so I’ll list a few random ones, I love following/watching boxing & I’ve messed with everything from music production, video editing, graphic design and more, I’m just a huge dreamer and I really want to achieve my goals! I can’t wait to come back here when I’m more financially free, able to help others & turned my hobbies into income!

- Do you have a favorite Frutiger Aero memory or moment?

Probably using an iPhone 4 for the first time, it was so ahead of its time and I loved how it all looked back then. Especially using an iPhone 4s, I thought Siri was the peak of humanity.

- If you could describe your artistic journey in three words, what would they be?

Overlooked, Underestimated, Relentless

- What advice would you give to aspiring artists who are inspired by Frutiger Aero?

Spend less time thinking and more time doing, start doing stuff because you like it. If you’re anything like me you probably overthink everything, get anxious about what people might think, become a perfectionist with things, then my best advice is to just DO things!! There are so many great artists, musicians, innovators who were seen as crazy or whatever art form they were doing was considered terrible by the masses and they aged like wine eventually. There’s so much power and growth that comes after slowly but surely just doing what you wanna do, behaving how you wanna behave, acting how you wanna act, regardless of what other people think. It’s very freeing and life is too short to worry about all that.
Another thing is that we all start somewhere. I know how incredibly demotivating it can be to see creating amazing pieces of media and feel like what you’re making is ass, but those people all started from nothing too. Everything that I am good at now I started out by making the shittiest pieces of video, graphic design, thumbnails, websites EVER… It took time and effort to get to where I am now and I still have so much to learn and grow from!
Also, once you start getting haters for no reason, just know you’re doing something right because you’re starting to get people jealous.

- Is there anything else you’d like to share with the Frutiger Aero community?

Just keep Frutiger Aero alive, take care of yourself!
