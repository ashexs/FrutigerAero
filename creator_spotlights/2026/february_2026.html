February 2026 Creator Spotlight - Perfect Hue

This month, our Artist of the Month is Perfect Hue!

Watch the YouTube short here.


Here's our interview with Perfect Hue:

- Can you introduce yourself and share a bit about your background?

My name is Jason aka Perfect Hue and I'm a designer & animator based in Colorado. I studied graphic design at MassArt in Boston and have over 20 years of freelance and full-time experience.
I made a lot of aero style wave wallpapers between 2006-2013 and some have been featured on devices like the Pantech Pursuit, Sky Vega, and Google Pixel.
Lately I've been doing a lot of 3d animation, motion graphics, and game development.

- What inspired you to become an artist?

Watching Dragon Ball Z as a kid and being amazed by the art style inspired me to start drawing. It was rewarding to make something I was proud of and could show to friends. I later got more into digital art through making AIM buddy icons, Flash animations, and web design.

- How did you first discover Frutiger Aero as an aesthetic, and what drew you to it?

Seeing the wave background on the PSP for the first time and wanting to create designs that captured the same vibe. It felt like the aesthetic of the future.

- How would you describe your art style?

Moody and atmospheric with bright saturated colors.

- Can you walk us through your creative process from idea to finished piece?

My wallpapers always started with a black canvas in Photoshop, and I would gradually add gradient shapes layer by layer to form the composition. Then I'd add my own digital photos, textures, and patterns to add more depth and uniqueness. Once it felt ready I would add photo techniques on top like cross processing effects, brightness/contrast, sharpening, grain, depth of field, etc. I also made everything much higher resolution than what the final output would be, so that the downscaling made it look even sharper. It took some effort to reduce artifacts like color banding, but usually worked around it by adding more texture. On average they consisted of 50-200 layers and took 1-2 weeks. I would distribute them through a handful of message boards, art portals, wallpaper blogs, and my own portfolio website.

- What tools or mediums do you prefer to use, and why?

Cinema 4D, Fl Studio, After Effects, and Photoshop. I can create basically anything I want with that combination.

- How do you overcome creative blocks?

Taking breaks and looking at things later with fresh eyes. Also having a steady work routine helps a lot.

- What inspires your work? Are there specific themes, emotions, or stories you aim to convey?

Futuristic and abstract moods, especially in movies or music. I'm also inspired by a lot of corporate design and architecture.

- Are there any artists, aesthetics, or movements that have influenced your style?

I first saw depthcore/metalheart wallpapers on the website Shadowness in 2003. A lot of my wallpaper design style comes from trying to mimic those pieces in Photoshop since 3D was too complicated for me when starting out. The names of my early wallpapers were based on Latin words which was a trend I had seen a lot.

- What role does the Frutiger Aero aesthetic play in your art?

I learned a lot of technical and visual skills when first creating aero designs that I still use today.

- What has been the most challenging piece you’ve created, and why?

Platform (2011) went through a lot of variations compared to previous designs I made. Outside of my aero work, my 3D animated short films I've made over the years have always taken a significant amount of time and effort.

- Can you share a piece you’re especially proud of and why it’s significant to you?

Hexillus (2007) made in Photoshop and Illustrator. This was the first design of mine that felt like a real, professional wallpaper. It also set the bar for my future designs and became a challenge to see if I could make something better.

- How has being part of the Frutiger Aero community influenced your artistic journey?

It's inspired me to get back into the aesthetic and explore more of the animation side of it. It's also cool to see so many people interested in an art style that wasn't as widely recognized or labeled at the time.

- What do you hope people take away from your art?

The same inspiration and motivation that my favorite art has given me.

- Do you have any upcoming projects or goals you’re excited about?

I recently launched a free game on Steam called Perfect Hue Arena, and I plan to continue adding more content to that and experimenting more with game development. It’s a fun medium that brings together all the different skills I’ve accumulated over the years.

- Where can people find more of your work or support you?

perfecthue.com or perfecthue on most platforms!

- If you could collaborate with any artist, living or dead, who would it be and why?

Ulrich Schnauss. His music resonates with me the most, especially his song (and album) "Goodbye"

- What’s a fun fact about you that most people wouldn’t know?

I spent a few hours on the set of the movie Skiptrace in Hong Kong and got to watch Jackie Chan film an action sequence!

- Do you have a favorite Frutiger Aero memory or moment?

Walking into an AT&T store and seeing my design on the Pantech Pursuit display unit.

- If you could describe your artistic journey in three words, what would they be?

Many fun projects

- What advice would you give to aspiring artists who are inspired by Frutiger Aero?

Recreating art you're inspired by is a good way to learn the tools and develop your own style.

- Is there anything else you’d like to share with the Frutiger Aero community?

I just started live streaming design/animation on Twitch on weekday mornings. Tune in if you have any requests or questions!
